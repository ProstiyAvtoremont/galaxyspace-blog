Surviving a Bear Market With Your Sense of Humor Intact

Every crypto bear market produces the same predictable cycle: panic, denial, gallows humor, memes, and eventually, for the people who stick around, a kind of weary affection for having survived it together. The gallows humor part is, weirdly, one of the most important stages — and crypto culture, Dogecoin spaces especially, has turned it into something close to an art form.

The Stages of Bear Market Humor

  1. Denial-phase jokes — "it's just a healthy correction," said with decreasing conviction as the chart keeps dropping.
  2. Acceptance-phase memes — the inevitable wave of self-deprecating content about portfolios, regret, and the specific shade of red everyone's exchange app turns during a crash.
  3. Community-bonding humor — jokes that stop being about the price entirely and start being about the shared experience of going through it together, which is usually the point where the humor gets genuinely funny instead of just bitter.
  4. The "we've seen this before" veteran humor — longtime holders making jokes specifically aimed at reassuring newer community members that this, too, has happened before and the community survived it.

Why This Matters More Than It Sounds Like It Should

Psychologists studying coping mechanisms have long identified humor as a legitimate, healthy coping strategy for stress, distinct from avoidance — shared laughter during difficult periods measurably reduces perceived stress and strengthens group bonds, according to research on social coping. Crypto communities that lean into this collective humor during downturns, rather than pure panic or silence, tend to retain members better through the hard periods than communities that don't.

Dogecoin's community in particular has leaned on this from its earliest days — a coin literally founded as a joke has a built-in cultural permission to laugh at itself, even during genuinely difficult market periods, in a way more self-serious crypto communities sometimes struggle to access.

Spending Through the Down Periods, Lightly

Bear markets are, understandably, not when most people feel like spending crypto on anything. But for the small, low-stakes, sentimental purchases — the kind that aren't about timing the market at all — a down period changes very little.
