The Greatest Prank Ever Pulled (As Voted by Group Chats)

Every long-running friend group has exactly one prank that achieves legendary status — the one that gets brought up at every reunion, referenced in half the group chat's inside jokes, and somehow gets slightly more elaborate in the retelling every single year, regardless of what actually happened.

Why These Stories Never Get Old

Comedy researchers studying humor and social bonding note that shared, well-told stories about group experiences — especially ones involving a clear "victim" who's still in on the joke — function as a kind of social glue, reinforcing group identity every time they're retold. The prank story isn't really about the prank anymore after a few years. It's about the group itself, and the fact that this specific set of people has shared history nobody else has access to.

The Universal Categories of Legendary Pranks

  • The slow-burn prank, set up weeks in advance, with a payoff so delayed the target had genuinely forgotten anything was coming
  • The prank that accidentally escalated, going further than anyone originally intended, usually due to one overenthusiastic participant
  • The prank that backfired spectacularly, becoming legendary specifically because the prankster ended up more embarrassed than the target
  • The prank involving an unwitting third party, a stranger, a sibling, or a pet who became an accidental co-conspirator

The Problem With Letting These Stories Live Only in Memory

Here's the issue: these stories get told constantly, but rarely written down anywhere, which means the actual details slowly drift with every retelling. This isn't necessarily a problem — exaggeration is half the fun — but it does mean the original, factual version of events tends to disappear within a few years, replaced by whichever version got the biggest laugh most recently.
